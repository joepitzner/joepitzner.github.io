Featured Project

Crocker Village - Alley Row - Residence 6

Petrovich Development / BlackPine Communities

An award-winning alley-loaded residence designed to bridge traditional neighborhood character with a modern urban lifestyle. The home draws on Spanish-influenced architecture, using proportion, material contrast, and detailing to create a sense of authenticity within a contemporary infill context.

A defining move was the integration of a private courtyard that anchors the plan - bringing natural light deep into the home while creating a seamless indoor-outdoor relationship between the great room, kitchen, and primary suite. This strategy enhances livability on a compact urban lot while maintaining privacy and openness.

Community Identity

Farmhouse at Willow Creek

BlackPine Communities

A 126-home residential community centered around a modern farmhouse architectural identity, designed to balance strong visual cohesion with flexibility across multiple floor plans.

The project emphasizes lifestyle-driven planning, with open-concept first floors tailored for entertaining and second-floor layouts that create separation between public and private space. Board-and-batten siding, metal roofing accents, and varied rooflines reinforce the community character while allowing individual homes to feel distinct.

BlackPine Communities

A 106-unit residential community that introduces a more urban, design-forward housing type to West Sacramento, blending density with livability.

Compact single-family homes were designed for efficiency without sacrificing spatial quality. Context + Density

The Creamery at Alkali Flat

BlackPine Communities

A 122-unit narrow-lot community designed to respond to the scale and character of Sacramento's historic Alkali Flat neighborhood while introducing a more contemporary residential product.

The homes are organized vertically to maximize livable space on constrained lots, incorporating flexible floor plans and strong indoor-outdoor connections. The design balances modern expression with contextual sensitivity, creating a neighborhood that feels both current and rooted in its surroundings.
